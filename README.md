# Bestiary 
Is a small university project.

## Task for the project
•	To create a console application by using С/С++ programming languages ( without using external databases or libraries ). Simplistic database management application that allows users to store and manage information about animals in a zoo

## Introduction
  I’ve chosen to call my app “Bestiary.” Bestiaries were books in the Middle Ages in which people wrote down their (at the time really limited) understanding of animals. There are fantastical stories about whales as big as islands covered with vegetation and birds feeding their chicks with their own blood.
I chose this because it sounds cool, and I love history, but also because it shows that humans have always been fascinated with animals. 
  Zoos have a long and complicated history, and the first public zoos were pretty terrible. At the time, keeping an animal alive for a few months was considered a big achievement.
Nowadays, it has changed a lot. Though there are still people who think of zoos as just a place where people can look at animals to fulfill their curiosity - something akin to a wax museum - they’re wrong. Zoos have many other goals, such as deepening our knowledge about animals and trying to preserve endangered species, almost always endangered because of human activity. We are also much more concerned about how to keep animals alive and happy, and for some animals (like orcas), that’s impossible in captivity. All this is possible because of the hard and coordinated work of hundreds of zoo workers.
As I’ll mention later, I picked this subject just because it was one of the first on the list, and I can’t honestly claim that my app contributes to this field yet. But maybe in the future, after more assignments like this and improving my programming knowledge, one day I could help in a real and meaningful way.
